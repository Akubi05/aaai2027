\section{Conclusion}
We studied whether frozen-VLM failures can guide representation editing without group annotations. \textsc{LatentRisk-VLM} uses task-label margins, class residualization, risk-direction estimation, and candidate-wise inference without updating either encoder. It discovers error-related latent risk directions, not sensitive attributes or demographic groups. Rank-1 editing substantially improves Waterbirds WGA, but harms CelebA WGA and trades retrieval utility for a modest FairFace skew reduction. Attribute-selected baselines remain stronger in settings where that supervision is available. Rank four offers no task-only advantage, while attribute-oracle selection exposes a large unresolved selection gap. Failure-derived directions are useful weak supervision when failure and hidden groups align, not a general substitute for group information.
